Two-way Binary Search

Binary search is a worst-case optimal algorithm for searching in a sorted sequence of elements.

  • A two-way binary search make [log2 n] + 1 two-way comparisons.
  • The recurrence is Cw(n)=Cw([n/2]) + 1 for n>1 ,Cw(1)=1.
  • The best case efficiency is not Θ(1), but rather Θ(log n). Why?
  • Advantage?
    • Early termination doesn’t save that much computation time, sometimes the removal of the equality comparison may be faster (always measure to confirm).
