Quick Sort Algorithm

Idea:
  • 1 Select a key from the array for which, we hope, about half the keys will come before and half after. Call this key the pivot.
  • 2 Partition the array so that all keys with a value less than the pivot are in one subarray, and larger keys come in the other subarray.
  • 3 Recursively apply the same procedure on the two subarrays separately.
  • 4 Put all the subarrays together, and the whole array will be in sorted order.
    • Quick sort does sorting in place (don’t need additional copying and temporary arrays).
    • Quick Sort is not a stable sorting method.
ALGORITHM QuickSort (A[l...r])
/* Sort a subarray using by quicksort. */
/* I N P U T : A subarray A[l . . . r ] of A[0 . . . n − 1], defined by its left and right indices l and r. */
/* OUTPUT : A subarray A[l...r] sorted in ascending order. */
  if l < r then
  /* s is the index to split array. */
  s = QPartition(A[l . . . r ])
  QuickSort(A[l . . . s − 1])
  QuickSort(A[s + 1 . . . r ])
  end if

  ALGORITHM QPartition (A[l . . . r])
  /* Partition a subarray using the first element as a pivot. */
  /* INPUT : A subarray A[l . . . r] of A[0 . . . n − 1], defined by its left
  and right indices l and r, where (l < r). */
  /* OUTPUT : A partition of A[l . . . r], along with the index of the split position. */
  p = A[l]
  i = l + 1;
  j = r;
  while i ≤j do
    while A[i] < p do
      i = i + 1 /* we assume there is some check to ensure we don’t go pass r */
    end while
    while A[j] > p do
      j=j−1
    end while
    swap(A[i ], A[j ])
  end while
  swap(A[i],A[j]) /* we need to reverse the last swap, which is incorrect when i and j could cross each other */
  swap(A[l],A[j])
  return j
