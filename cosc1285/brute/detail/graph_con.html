Bubble sort

  • A graph is a pair ⟨V,E⟩, where
    • V is a set of nodes, called vertices.
    • E is a collection of pairs of vertices, called edges.
  • A graph G is undirected if all of the pairs of vertices in E are unordered.
  • A graph G is directed if all of the pairs of vertices in E have an ordering imposed.
  • A graph G with every pair of its vertices connected by an edge is called complete, often denoted K|V|.
  • The degree of a vertex is the number of incident edges of the vertex.
  • ApathfromvertexutovertexvofagraphGisasequenceof adjacent (connected by an edge) vertices that starts with u and ends with v. If all the vertices are distinct, the path is said to be simple.
  • The length of a path is the total number of vertices in a vertex sequence defining its path minus one, which is the same as the number of edges in the path.
  • A graph is said to be connected if for every pair of vertices u and v there is a path from u to v.
